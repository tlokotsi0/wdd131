History of Semonkong

Semonkong, meaning "Place of Smoke", is a historic village nestled in the heart of Lesotho. Here's a glimpse into its past: Origins: Semonkong was established in the 1880s as a refuge for Basotho people displaced during the Basuto Gun War. This conflict saw the Basotho people resisting the Cape Colony's attempts to disarm them. The village provided a sanctuary for those fleeing the war, contributing to its growth and development.

Semonkong is renowned for its proximity to the majestic Maletsunyane Falls, one of the highest single-drop waterfalls in the world. The falls create a misty "smoke" that gives the village its name. Historically, Semonkong's remote location contributed to its unique character. It served as a hideout for outlaws and retained an untamed atmosphere. Today, Semonkong is a popular destination for tourists seeking adventure and natural beauty. It offers activities like pony trekking, hiking, and exploring the stunning Maletsunyane Falls. The village also provides a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of the Basotho people.
